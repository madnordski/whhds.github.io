9/20/99 Regular season over/Alternate Ride Schedule approved!
I'm sad to report the end of the regular WHHDS season. However, the WHHDS alternative ride schedule for reprobates and retirees was unanimously approved by the board of directors and is now officially posted:

Meet at the Mills St. entrace of the arboretum Monday/Wednesday/Friday at 8:15 or Tuesday/Thursday at 7:45. Rides are to Paoli.

Though this is an "alternative" ride, you needn't lead an alternative lifestyle to join the group. Piercings, tattoos, and baggie pants are strictly optional.

This will be the last update to this site until next spring. I promise to put a little more energy into it than I have the last few weeks of this season!

9/2/99 Darkness/Sprints
Well, gentle reader, time is running out on the '99 edition of the WHHDS morning rides. We lose more and more light every day and have actually pushed our rides back to 6:15 until the end of the season.

In these closing weeks RickA and DamonB have declared themselves in shape enough to start sprinting up to the beltline on the return trip. As of 9/2/99, Rick leads 3-2. We still have six more rides left, so anything can happen.

Big news, a new rider, Jeff from Maryland, has joined in the past couple of weeks. He's here in Madison to work on a Phd. in something to do with education. Apparently he met DonaldC at a weekly Friday afternoon "seminar" on the Union Terrace.

WHHDS member DamonB held the Second Annual, First Actual Hill Ride to Hell in early August starting from Salmo Pond. The ride was a little over 50 miles with 9 significant climbs and plenty of good descents. Eleven brave riders tackled the hills and most found the ride to be too short and maybe not quite hilly enough to be considered a hill ride to hell. Well, wait until next year, is all I have to say about that. Here's the route.

There's talk afoot about the end-of-year ride, picnic, and croquet tournament. More news to follow...

7/30/99 Are those socks or what? -- Morning cobwebs
Well, yours truly has been remiss on mentioning another new member for this year-- none other than RickA's own baby brother ChrisT! The reason I never mentioned him is because he's been threatening to join us for the last three years, so when he finally turned up one morning it was as if we'd known him all along. Forgive me Chris and welcome to the ride.

Of course now that I've mentioned him, I just have to pick on him a little bit. Not one to bother with the latest in cycling gear and fashion, ChrisT was quite the fashion plate on the morning of 7/27. No, he wasn't wearing fancy black Pearl Izumi socks, or stylish yellow socks a la the USPS team riding down the Champs-Elysées with Lance Armstrong in tow. No, it looked as if he tore the sleeves off an old ratty t-shirt and slid the ever-so-loosely over his ankles. Not a bit of elastic. I'd hate to see how long he goes before replacing his underwear.

On the morning of 7/28 your fearless webmaster was waiting alone on the Mills Street Bridge for the ride to begin when ChrisT rides up, says hello, and kept riding on past thinking he's missed the ride. Snubbed, DamonB eventually chased him down (after thoughtfully saying hello to morning runner JoanS) to admonish this wretched behaviour. Turns out Chris said he didn't recognize me because I was wearing a different jersey. He thought to himself, "Hmmmm, that's not SteveR waiting there, so I must have missed the ride." Now, mind you, SteveR shows up about 3 times a month. What am I, chopped liver?

Later, Chris explained that he must have had cobwebs in his brain because of the early hour.

Finally for this week, the no sprinting rule will remain in effect until we are in shape. Maybe by mid-September or so... Of course, I say this only after RickA thoroughly dusted me (twice!) on a "friendly" Sunday ride.

7/15/99 Heart Healthy Breakfast/Tofu dogs/New member!
The big highlight of the past couple of weeks was the first annual heart- healthy blueberry breakfast graciously hosted by Rick and PatF. Wonderful Ripe blueberries, granola, juice, and coffee were prepared for the WHHDS members after a typically good ride, and we were all off to work by 8:15... or so.

During the breakfast RickF presented vegie-boy DamonB a package of Tofu-wieners. According to Rick, he saw them in the store and just had to buy them. Damon would like to report that the wieners were good. A little mustard, a little relish, and they went down fine.

The breakfast also welcomed a new regular rider, Michelle, who has been upping our average daily attendence since she signed on about two weeks ago. She's headed up to Duluth this weekend for a bit of racing so we're all looking forward to hearing about her trip.

On a sad note, DonaldC announced that it's time to wax up the skis and sharpen the skates--the days are getting shorter.

Oh, the UCI pressured the WHHDS into letting Donald ride frisky again. Apparently there was some sort of mistake in the testing.

6/25/99 New Members.../Heron siting/Web page honors
It's been a quiet week on Lake Wingra. While DonaldC still pleads his case with the UCI in France, RickA, RickF, and DamonB spent the week recruiting a couple of folks for the morning ride. Welcoming committee member DamonB finally chased down the fellow we see daily riding back and forth through the arboretum in an unbelievably huge gear. He's been a morning regular for a couple of years now but has never joined the WHHDS. On the morning of 6/24 a racer from the Great Dane Cycling team was invited to ride with us but it's unclear yet if she'll take up the offer to ride with the has-been racers, retirees, and riff-raff that make up the WHHDS. (Some of us fit into more than one category...)

On 6/15/99 a heron was sited on the far shore of Lake Wingra near the zoo. A nice way to start the day. We're looking forward to seeing more as the summer wears on. In past years we've been lucky enough to see herons on the near shore.

NEWSFLASH! Our humble web page made the spotlight section of the Bicycle Community Page. We always thought our page was special. Glad to see that others do too. Now if we could only get a few more regular morning riders...

6/11/99 Motion approved/Paris/Primordial Ooze
The Whalen/Mutchler motion was unanimously approved so on week 7 we added the extra leg to the ride, extending our daily outing by a couple of miles.

Still smarting from his censuring, DonaldC took off for Europe to plead his case to the UCI, who, of late have been d ealing with other riders in similar quandaries. Will he admit to EPO usage, or was it just his beta-blockers?

What do you get when your deep freezer goes on the fritz three weeks before you discover the mess? Ask member RickF about the primordial ooze soup in the basement deep freeze. It's been two days now and he apparently still hasn't figured out how to clean up the mess. Sounds like his wife has volunteered to clean it up. Yet another shining example of how women have way more intestinal fortitude than men.

TOMRV is this weekend and the WHHDS is sending RickA as club representative. He'll be joining many Wednesday Night bikers on this annual pilgrimage to Iowa. DamonB will probably go next year because he can't find anyone to ride with around Madison this weekend.

5/28/99 General news and a Motion before the Board of Directors
Week 5 was rainy or otherwise sparsely attended as some of our members were out of town. RickA traveled to Jackson, Wyoming ostensibly to accompany his wife on a business trip, but some members think he went specifically for the altitude training prior to the upcoming sprinting season. DonaldC traveled to LA to visit his daughter, but again, other members of the group postulated that he was really trying to escape the shame and embarrassment of his recent censuring.

Week 6 showed solid growth in numbers and ride vigor prompting a motion to be put before the board of directors:

Webmaster DamonB proposed that starting June 1st, we add the Whalen/Mutchler leg to our ride. He says, "The rides are progressing nicely in terms of participation and intensity, therefore it's time to add the extra leg to the ride and to, as chef Emeril Lagasse says, kick it up a notch." The board is considering the motion and will report its findings next week.

4/30/99 Cold weather, numb hands, low turnout
The first two weeks of the WHHDS rides produced mixed results. Charter member DonaldC abandoned most of week 1 because of low temps, while the always-cheerful RickF decided that two days was all he cared to suffer through before taking off on a trip to sunny Florida. Can't wait until he returns on Monday all softened up by 90 degree Florida temps. Heh, heh, heh. RickA managed only one ride that first week but decided to make up for it by riding all five days during week 2--probably the only time this year he'll manage not to shut the alarm off in his sleep for an entire week.

Week 2 was mostly an easy week attended daily by DamonB and RickA and joined by SteveR for one day of hard riding. Wednesday Night regular GregA showed up briefly on Friday and promptly jacked up the pace to 24 until he turned off at Whalen. Rick and Damon sensibly toned it down to 17mph for the rest of the ride. You won't be showing up regularly will you Greg?

It looks like warmer weather is on the way so we should be back up to full strength in the coming weeks.

Because of low turn out, the no-sprinting rule will be in effect for at least another week.
