WHHDS Rule Amendments
published in accordance with club charter and bylaws

Amendment One: The Five Hour Rule
If a rider in good standing does not get at least five hours of sleep, rider is granted the day off.

Amendment Two: The Hard Ride the Night Before Rule
If a rider in good standing has a hard ride the night before a scheduled WHHDS event, rider is granted the day off. Note: This one only goes over a few times.

Amendment Three: The Sprinting and Fitness Rule
Although sprints are scheduled to begin two weeks into the season, sprinters reserve the right to withhold sprinting until an adequate level of fitness is achieved by all sprinting parties.

Amendment Four: The Good Standing Rule
Otherwise known as the Robert's Rule, this rule is designed to better define a rider in good standing. A rider is considered to be in good standing only if said rider participates in 40% of the rides. That is, the rider must show up at least two days per week during the regular riding season. Riders in Good Standing are eligible for many of the benefits associated with the WHHDS. See the ride captain for details.

Amendment Five: The Silver Mane Rule
Riders who have grandchildren and/or riders receiving Social Security benefits, may take any day off without notice and will receive no disciplinary sanctions.
