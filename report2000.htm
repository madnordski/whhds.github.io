9/25/00 Guest Column, Final notes
This final Year 2000 Ride Report comes to you from ChrisT describing the last morning ride of the year:

"Fall is here, time to wax the skis.
The last morning ride of the season took place on Friday. The Board of Directors* failed to make an appearance for the finale, leaving GregA, Don N., SharonH, LindaZ and ChrisT to fend for themselves. Aside from a few wrong turns, some bumping and pushing in the pack, and a few crazed motorists, the ride was relatively uneventful.

The ride ended with some "hot from the oven" apple pastries. Rumor has it, that Linda scoured her neighborhood at 3:30 AM in search of the freshest apples and began baking at 4:30. Thank you, again.

Rumor also has it, that Damon missed the ride to attend his "poetry for bicyclists" class** (or was that "yoga for poets"?). We all look forward to Damon's poetry at the awards banquet... "

*DonaldC responds to comments regarding the Board of Directors:
"Thanks to Chris for his report on the final ride of the season. Given his remark about the absence of directors on the ride, this might be a good time to clarify any confusion on this matter.

"I'm not sure who the directors are. I receive communications from them from time to time indicating decisions they have made, schedules and schedule changes, rules, and so forth. Each fall they send me a package of jerseys and the list of team members who are to receive them. I'm not sure whether they (the directors) communicate with other team members or not, nor do I know whether or not any team members are directors.

That's all I know. They seem to have chosen me as a messenger, but there may be other messengers. I invite you all to share news of any contacts you have had with directors."

**DamonB responds to comments regarding Poetry for Bicyclists:
That would be "Yoga for Cyclists", taught by the able and flexible Debra Thorne of Oasis Yoga. After only two classes, there has been significant flexibility gains in the hip and buttock regions, however, breathing with full awareness and without judgement is still somewhat of a problem.

Regardless, I liked the idea of "Poetry for Cyclists" so I'm currently working on some verse for the picnic and awards banquet in October.

That about wraps it up for 2000. Once again, it's been a banner year for the Wingra, Heron, Hops, and Derrailleur Society. We've shown significant growth and diversity this year, and the BOD is planning for more of the same next summer. See you then.

8/10/00 Award ceremonies, Heart Healthy Breakfast, The Man Ride
July's been a busy month. We've held several post-ride award ceremonies, most notably the presentaion of the Maillot Saindoux (lard jersey) to RickF, the special member inductee presentation to Brian, and the Canned Meat awards following the Heart Healthy Breakfast at RickF's home.

As mentioned in a previous post, RickF has had a 17-year, greasy-fingered choke hold on the Maillot Saindoux, and this year was duly rewarded with the FAT Man jersey from Velo Gear. His wife questioned him if he was okay with it. She just doesn't understand.

Proud new WHHDS member BrianX received the club jersey early this week, just in time for him to move to Idaho to fight fires, and go to law school. Brian, it's been a pleasure! Welcome to the club. So long.

RickF held his second annual Heart Healthy Breakfast the morning of 8/10. Granola, yoghurt, blueberry pancakes, melon, and coffee. Absolutely great. Definitely better than riding. Rick raided the local Open Pantry for some canned meat products for an informal awards ceremony. I'm not sure I get the significance, so we'll have to discuss it at our next board meeting.

The Tuesday 8/8 morning ride was somewhat of a blast from the past. Only (only!) five members showed up and most notably there were no women. This marked the first all-male ride in several months and was quickly dubbed "The Man Ride." We talked about fishing and hunting, cigars, ab work outs, fast cars, and football (American Football). It felt as if we were all part of a living, breathing men's magazine. By the end of the ride, we all decided it was a great ride but that we missed having women along. In a brief, but moving, drumming and chanting ceremony, we promised to get together for another Man Ride soon.

5/1/00 The season has begun
After our typical spring of fits and starts, we've begun our morning rides in earnest. The last week of April turned out to be beautiful, albeit a little cold. The first of May was a rainout, but the weather forecast looks promising for the rest of the week.

On Friday of last week, BillD showed up for what we hope to be a more than his only ride of the year. Last year Bill showed up for one ride all season never to be seen again. Welcome back Bill.

The weather is probably just a little cold yet for ChrisT to start showing up. As you may recall, Chris "not a slave to fashion" Thiel doesn't invest heavily in the cycling apparel department regardless of the season. I'm sure we'll see him in just a few weeks and we're all looking forward to his regular appearance.

RickF announced yesterday that he's down to 194 pounds of seething muscle. He's the club leader in the "maillot saindoux" competition and looks forward to continuing his unprecedented 17 year strangle-hold on that jersery.

WHHDS members DamonB and RickF participated in the Wisconsin State Doubles Squash tournament this past weekend and were embarassed off the court in the first round. It's a good thing biking season is here because neither of them want to talk about squash with anyone ever again. Ever.
